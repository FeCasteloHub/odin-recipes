Brownie

Description

A brownie is a dense, chocolatey baked dessert that originated in the United States. It has a soft, fudgy interior with a slightly crisp top, striking a balance between a cookie and a cake. Brownies can be customized with add-ins like walnuts, pecans, or chocolate chips, and are often served warm, sometimes with a scoop of vanilla ice cream. They're a staple in American baking and a favorite comfort food around the world.

Ingredients

  • 150g dark chocolate (about 1 cup, chopped)
  • 100g unsalted butter (1/2 cup)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Optional:1/2 cup chopped walnuts or chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F). Line a square baking pan with parchment paper.
  2. Melt chocolate and butter together in a saucepan over low heat. Let cool slightly.
  3. Stir in sugar, then eggs one at a time, mixing well. Add vanilla.
  4. Fold in flour and salt until just combined. Add nuts or chocolate chips if using.
  5. Pour into prepared pan and smooth the top.
  6. Bake for 20–25 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out with moist crumbs.
  7. Let cool before cutting into squares.
